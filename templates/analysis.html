Factors Impacting Adults Obesity

Click on each plot for further details

Limited Access to Healthy Foods 2020

Limited access to healthy foods is positively associated with the rates of obesity in American adults. Decreased access to healthy foods caused a trend of increased obesity across the United States. For every 10% decrease in access to healthy foods, adult obesity increases by 7%.


Food Environment Index 2020

The food environment index (FEI) is an indicator of the factors that contribute to a healthy food environment with ‘0’ being the worst and ‘10’ being the best. Rates of adult obesity in the United States showed a decreasing trend with increasing FEI.


Physically Inactive 2020

Greater physical inactivity has a moderately positive linear association with rates of adult obesity in the United States. A 10% increase in physical inactivity was associated with an approximately 7% increase in adult obesity.


Access to Exercise Opportunities 2020

Greater access to exercise opportunities has a moderate negative association with adult obesity in the United States. A 10% increase in access to exercise opportunities resulted in a 2.5% decrease in obesity across the country.


High School Graduation Rate 2020

There does not appear to be any significant relationship between high school graduation rates and prevalence of adult obesity.


College Education Rate 2020

There is no association between college education and prevalence of obesity in adults in the United States.


Expenditure per Capita by Fast-food Restaurants 2020

There is no association between the amount of expenditure by fast-food restaurants and adult obesity rates in the United States.


Expenditure per capita by Restaurants 2012

Expenditure per capita for non-fast food restaurants had a weak negative association with adult obesity rates in the United States in 2012.


Direct Farm Sales per Capita 2012

There is no association between direct farm sales per capita and adult obesity rates in the United States.


Unemployed 2020

There is no association between % unemployment across the United States and prevalence of adult obesity.


Income ratio 2020

The income ratio indicates the ratio of median household income at the 80th percentile compared to income at the 20th percentile. There is no association between the income ratio and the prevalence of obesity in the United States.


Median Household Income 2020

Median household income has a weak negative association with adult obesity in the United States. In general, as median household income increases, adult obesity rates tend to decrease.


Median Household Income Asian 2020

There is no association between median household income for any specific race, Asian, Hispanic, Black, or White, in the United States and prevalence of adult obesity.


Median Household Income Hispanic 2020

There is no association between median household income for any specific race, Asian, Hispanic, Black, or White, in the United States and prevalence of adult obesity.


Median Household Income Black 2020

There is no association between median household income for any specific race, Asian, Hispanic, Black, or White, in the United States and prevalence of adult obesity.


Median Household Income White 2020

There is no association between median household income for any specific race, Asian, Hispanic, Black, or White, in the United States and prevalence of adult obesity.
