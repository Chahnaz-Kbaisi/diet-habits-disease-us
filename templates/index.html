Highlights

This project investigates the role of American dietary trends and the impact of healthy food availability on the prevalence of obesity and diabetes in the United States. Moreover, the analysis considers lifestyle choices like physical activity and several socioeconomic factors like education level and income to determine whether they are associated with disease rates. The overview and objectives below provide a more complete description.


Overview

The United States has a diverse population in terms of demographics, socioeconomic status (SES), lifestyle, and living conditions. These factors play a role in the general health of each person and potentially contribute to subsequent risk of disease. In particular, diabetes and obesity cause a significant social and economic burden on the United States; however, the role of certain modifying factors in their prevalence remains unclear.
