Overview

Americans constitute a diverse population of individuals and various demographic subgroups, not only in terms of race and ethnicity, but also socioeconomic status (SES), lifestyle, and living conditions. These factors determine the general health and well-being of each person, and the subsequent risk of disease. In particular, chronic diseases like diabetes and obesity cause a significant social and economic burden on the United States, and although a vast amount of data has already been collected about their etiology, the role of certain modifying factors in their prevalence remains unclear.

Adapted from The Milken Institute, Weighing America Down, The Health & Economic Impact of Obesity, 2016
